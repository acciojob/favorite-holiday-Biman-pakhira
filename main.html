Ladakh

Ladakh is a mountainous region in India known as the "land of high mountain passes," characterized by high-altitude deserts, rugged mountains, deep valleys, and a stark but beautiful landscape. It is situated in the Himalayas and bordered by the Karakoram and Zanskar ranges, with the Indus being the most significant river. The region is also a center of Tibetan-Buddhist culture, evident in its numerous monasteries, and is popular for adventure and its stunning natural scenery, which includes clear lakes like Pangong and Tso Moriri
